K2-356b

K2-356b is so hot that the heat tears molecules apart on the dayside.

K2-356 b is a Neptune-like exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 5.86 Earths, it takes 21 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.1599 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2021.

K2-356 b is a Neptune-like exoplanet discovered in 2021, orbiting the star K2-356. This planet falls into the category of gas giants, similar in composition to Neptune but smaller in size. Its mass is approximately 5.86 times that of Earth, and its radius is around 2.29 times that of Earth (about 0.204 Jupiter radii). Due to its proximity to its host star, K2-356 b completes one full orbit in just 21 days, much faster than Earth's orbit around the Sun.

Located at a distance of 0.1599 AU from its star, K2-356 b is relatively close, leading to high temperatures with an estimated equilibrium temperature of 624 K (351°C). The planet was detected using the transit method, where astronomers observe the dimming of the star's light as the planet passes in front of it. This method, applied through the Kepler space telescope's K2 mission, allowed scientists to learn more about the planet's characteristics

The star K2-356 is a G-type star, similar to our Sun, but located approximately 1160 light-years from Earth. K2-356 b's close orbit places it well outside the habitable zone, meaning it's far too hot to support life as we know it. Nonetheless, its discovery adds to our understanding of the diversity of exoplanets, especially gas giants like Neptune in close orbits​
