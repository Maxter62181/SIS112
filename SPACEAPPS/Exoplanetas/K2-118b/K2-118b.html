K2-118 b is a Neptune-like exoplanet that orbits a K-type star. Discovered in 2017, the planet has a mass of approximately 6.99 Earth masses and a radius about 22.7% the size of Jupiter. K2-118 b completes its orbit around the star every 50.9 days, with an orbital distance of 0.245 AU. It has an eccentricity of 0.2, indicating that its orbit is slightly elliptical. The planet was identified using the transit method, which observes the dimming of a star as a planet passes in front of it
