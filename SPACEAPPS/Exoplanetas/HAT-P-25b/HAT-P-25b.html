HAT-P-25b

HAT-P-25 b is a gas giant exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 0.569 Jupiters, it takes 3.7 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.0466 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2010.

HAT-P-25b is a gas giant exoplanet that orbits a G-type star known as HAT-P-25. The planet was discovered in 2010 through the transit method, which detects planets by observing the slight dimming of a star as a planet passes in front of it. HAT-P-25b is classified as a "hot Jupiter" due to its large size and close proximity to its host star.

The planet has a mass of about 0.57 times that of Jupiter, and its radius is approximately 1.19 times Jupiter's radius. It orbits its star at a very short distance of 0.0466 AU, which is much closer than Mercury is to the Sun. As a result, HAT-P-25b completes an orbit in just 3.65 days and has an estimated equilibrium temperature of around 1100 K (827°C), making it an extremely hot planet.

The host star, HAT-P-25, is located about 988 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Aries. It has a mass similar to the Sun, but slightly smaller with a radius of about 0.96 times that of the Sun. The star is enriched in metals compared to the Sun, which may have contributed to the formation of its giant planet.
