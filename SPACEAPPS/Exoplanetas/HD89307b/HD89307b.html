HD 89307 b is a gas giant exoplanet that orbits a G-type star. Its mass is 2.02 Jupiters, it takes 5.9 years to complete one orbit of its star and is 3.27 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2009.

HD 89307 b is a gas giant exoplanet discovered in 2009, orbiting the G-type star HD 89307, which is located approximately 104 light-years away in the constellation Leo. The planet is classified as a gas giant similar to Jupiter, with a mass around 2.02 times that of Jupiter and an estimated radius of 1.19 times Jupiter's radius.

HD 89307 b has an orbital period of about 5.9 years, meaning it takes nearly six years to complete one orbit around its host star. The planet orbits at an average distance of 3.27 AU (Astronomical Units), placing it about three times farther from its star than Earth is from the Sun. Its orbit has a slight eccentricity of 0.2, indicating that it follows a somewhat elongated path around its star.

This exoplanet was detected using the radial velocity method, which measures the wobble of the host star caused by the gravitational pull of the orbiting planet
