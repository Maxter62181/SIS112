TOI-1696 b is a recently discovered gas giant exoplanet that orbits a cool M-type star, located approximately 212 light-years away in the constellation of Aries. It was identified using the transit method and announced in 2022.

This planet has a relatively low mass, about 0.153 times the mass of Jupiter, and a radius of about 3.09 times that of Earth, placing it in the category of sub-Neptune-sized planets. It orbits its host star at an incredibly short distance of 0.0229 AU (around 2% of the Earth-Sun distance) and completes one full orbit in just 2.5 days. Due to its close proximity to its star, TOI-1696 b experiences extreme temperatures, with an estimated equilibrium temperature of around 489 K (216°C).

The planet is of particular interest because it lies in the so-called "Neptunian desert," a region where planets of its size and type are rare. Its discovery provides valuable data for studying the atmospheric composition and formation processes of small gas giants, especially those orbiting M-dwarf stars​
