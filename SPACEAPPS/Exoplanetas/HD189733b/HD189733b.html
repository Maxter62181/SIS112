HD189733b

This far-off blue planet may look like a friendly haven – but don’t be deceived! Weather here is deadly. The planet’s cobalt blue color comes from a hazy, blow-torched atmosphere containing clouds laced with glass.

HD 189733 b is a well-known exoplanet that orbits the star HD 189733, located about 64.5 light-years away in the constellation Vulpecula. Discovered in 2005 using the transit method, this planet is classified as a "hot Jupiter" due to its large size and close proximity to its host star. It is roughly 13% larger than Jupiter, with a mass about 1.14 times Jupiter's mass. HD 189733 b is notable for its short orbital period, completing a full orbit around its star in just 2.2 days.

The planet's atmosphere has drawn significant attention due to its extreme conditions. Observations using the Hubble Space Telescope and Spitzer Space Telescope have revealed that HD 189733 b has intense weather patterns, including violent winds exceeding 5,400 miles per hour (8,700 km/h). The planet's temperature can soar to over 1,000°C (1,832°F) due to its proximity to its star, making it a blisteringly hot world.

One of the most striking discoveries about HD 189733 b is its atmospheric composition. Spectroscopic analysis has shown the presence of sodium, methane, water vapor, and carbon dioxide, making it a prime candidate for studying exoplanet atmospheres. Additionally, the planet's atmosphere is thought to contain silicate particles, which may contribute to its deep blue appearance, resembling Earth's sky but with much more extreme and stormy weather
