DP Leonis b is a gas giant exoplanet that orbits the binary star system DP Leonis, which consists of a white dwarf and a red dwarf. Discovered in 2009, this planet is notable for being a circumbinary planet, meaning it orbits both stars in the system.

The planet has a mass of approximately 6.28 times that of Jupiter, making it a gas giant. Its orbit is elliptical, with an eccentricity of 0.39, and it takes 28 years to complete one orbit around the binary system. The semi-major axis of its orbit is 8.19 AU, placing it far from its host stars. The discovery of DP Leonis b was made using eclipse timing variations, a method that analyzes the timing shifts in the eclipses of the stars to detect the gravitational influence of a third body, such as a planet​

The central binary system, DP Leonis, is located around 990 light-years away in the constellation of Leo. The system is of particular interest due to its classification as a cataclysmic variable star, with frequent eclipses caused by the interaction between the white dwarf and its companion star​
