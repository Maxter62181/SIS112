Kepler-974b

Kepler-974 b is a super Earth exoplanet that orbits an M-type star. Its mass is 3.09 Earths, it takes 4.2 days to complete one orbit of its star, and is 0.0419 AU from its star. Its discovery was announced in 2016.

Kepler-974 b is a super-Earth exoplanet discovered in 2016 as part of NASA's Kepler mission. It orbits an M-type star (a cool, red dwarf) located about 212 light-years away. This planet has a mass of around 3.09 times that of Earth and a radius approximately 1.57 times Earth's radius, classifying it as a super-Earth. It orbits very close to its star, with a distance of only 0.0419 AU (about 4% of the distance between the Earth and the Sun), completing one orbit in just 4.2 days.

Kepler-974 b is also intriguing because it's considered a candidate for being an iron-rich planet, potentially composed largely of metallic elements. Its extreme proximity to the host star results in high surface temperatures, which make it inhospitable to life as we know it. Its discovery contributes to our understanding of planetary composition diversity, particularly in planets that may have metallic cores similar to, but far larger than, Earth
