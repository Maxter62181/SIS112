HD40307g

HD 40307 g is a fascinating exoplanet discovered in 2012, located around 42 light-years away in the constellation Pictor. It orbits a K-type dwarf star called HD 40307, which is smaller and cooler than our Sun. This planet is part of a six-planet system, but what makes it particularly intriguing is that it lies within the habitable zone of its star, where conditions could allow liquid water to exist, increasing the potential for life.

Classified as a super-Earth, HD 40307 g is about 7 times more massive than Earth and orbits its star at a distance of 0.6 AU (about 90 million km), taking roughly 197.8 days to complete one orbit. Its size and mass suggest it could either have a rocky surface or a thick atmosphere like Neptune, but this is still uncertain. One interesting aspect of this planet is that it might not be tidally locked to its star, meaning it could have day-night cycles similar to Earth, which would help distribute heat more evenly across the planet’s surface, possibly supporting temperate conditions.
